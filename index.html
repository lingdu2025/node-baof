Why Protect Flamingos?

Flamingos are more than beautiful birds—they’re indicators of wetland health and ecological balance.

Graceful Guardians of Wetlands

Flamingos filter-feed on algae and small crustaceans, helping regulate aquatic ecosystems. Their pink color comes from carotenoids in their diet—a sign of a thriving environment.

Yet, habitat loss, pollution, climate change, and illegal egg collection threaten their survival. Some populations have declined by over 40% in the last two decades.

Since 2015, Flamingo Guardians has protected nesting sites, restored wetlands, and educated communities across Africa, the Caribbean, and South America.

Threats to Flamingos

Understanding the dangers is the first step to stopping them.

Habitat Loss

Wetlands drained for agriculture, urban development, and mining destroy critical nesting and feeding grounds.

Climate Change

Rising temperatures and altered rainfall patterns disrupt breeding cycles and reduce food availability.

Pollution & Poaching

Toxic runoff contaminates water, while illegal egg collection and hunting persist in vulnerable regions.
